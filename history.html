OUR FAMILY, OUR HISTORY

On January 1, 1965, the Ambassador Restaurant opened under the new ownership of the Rossi family, and transformed the space from a tap bar into a restaurant that specialized in pizza and sandwiches.

In 1978, the Ambassador was expanded into the space next door, and the second dining room was added. In January 2007, the Ambassador became a non-smoking establishment.

The Ambassador Restaurant, has been owned and managed by the Rossi family since 1965. The building has historical significance for the city of Houghton, having been built originally by Bosch Brewing Company in the mid 1800’s, which catered to the local miners who worked the copper mines.

Bosch Brewing had built the Ambassador as a tap bar to showcase their local brews. When the family took over the operation of the bar, they expanded the business to include a full menu restaurant highlighting pizza as a main menu item. Since that time, the pizza offerings have increased to include specialty pizza and also an extensive alcoholic beverage menu as added.

THE AMBASSADOR



To explain the history of the Ambassador and the unique murals that line the interior walls, the family conducted research and wrote a poemdetailing the story. The poem, entitled "Come Fill a Bumper," has since been printed on the cover of the Ambassador menu.

The first line of the poem mentions "Mr. Rohrbeck," who was “given a job to do.” Rohrbeck, the mural artist, was commissioned by Joseph Bosch (born in Baden, Germany in 1850) to paint the murals specifically for the Bosch brewery. The murals were later placed in the "oldGiltedge Bar." Featuring gnomes in various states of celebration, the murals are oil on canvas, with several coats of varnish on top for preservation. The murals were eventually sold to the Rossi family, and today, cover the walls of the Ambassador.
